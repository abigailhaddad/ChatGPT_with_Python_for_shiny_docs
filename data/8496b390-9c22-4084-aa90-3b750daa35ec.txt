         A federal grand jury has charged Donald Trump with 31 counts of willfully retaining national defense secrets in violation of the Espionage Act. Trump says the indictment is “the most horrible abuse of power” and a “hoax.” He should watch
         
          HBO’s docudrama “Reality,”
         
         which assiduously walks through the
         
          arrest by Trump’s Justice Department of Reality Winner
         
         , the National Security Agency translator who leaked one classified document and was sentenced to more than five years in prison.
        

         Winner was a contractor who saw a classified NSA report detailing Russian attempts to interfere in the 2016 election via a cyberattack on a voting software supplier and phishing emails aimed at local election officials. She sent it to
         
          The Intercept
         
         , which made it public.
        







             Advertisement
            















             Former President Donald Trump waves after speaking at the Georgia Republican convention, Saturday, June 10, 2023, in Columbus, Ga. (AP Photo/John Bazemore)
            
            (John Bazemore/AP)
           




         The leak was a violation of the law, and for it, Winner paid a steep price. For exposing one secret document that gave the American people information it likely had a right to know, the Air Force vet — who pleaded guilty —
         
          got the longest sentence ever imposed for the unauthorized release of government information to the media
         
         . Speaking to the judge upon her sentencing, she took “full responsibility” for the “undeniable mistake” and “would like to apologize profusely.” (Winner was released from prison into home confinement in 2021.)
        

         What about Trump, who illegally
         
          retained 140 classified documents
         
         in his Florida residence and then obfuscated rather than cooperating with federal officials who wanted them back? The special counsel’s evidence includes an audio recording in which Trump, speaking about a secret document in 2021, says
         
          “As president, I could have declassified it, but now I can’t”
         
         — a critical admission that he understood that what he was doing was unlawful.
